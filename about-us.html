About Peptide Club

format_quote

Peptide Club started in February 2026 as an idea between two students — Zeb and Gaurav.

We'd both been deep in the research chemicals space for a while, reading studies, sourcing peptides, and navigating a world that had very little honest guidance in it. The information was either buried in dense research papers, scattered across forums, or dressed up in marketing language that glossed over the important details. For anyone approaching peptides seriously — for research purposes — there was no clean, straightforward place to start.

So we built one.

Peptide Club exists to educate and consult around peptides as research chemicals. Everything we offer is intended strictly for research and educational purposes. We're not here to make medical claims — we're here to make the science accessible and the sourcing conversation more transparent for those who are already in this space.

We started as two students who got tired of the noise. We still are. A few months in, we won't pretend we have it all figured out — but we know this space well, we've done the work ourselves, and we're committed to being a resource people can actually trust.

This is still early. But the reason we started hasn't changed.

— Zeb & Gaurav

Co-Founders of Peptide Club
